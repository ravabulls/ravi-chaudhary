# Supply Chain Digitization

## The Shift to Digital SCM

Current **Supply Chain Management (SCM)** is undergoing a fundamental transformation driven by two forces:

- **High data availability** — from operations, purchases, and payments
- **The Platform Economy** — reshaping how businesses connect and transact

This creates a core tension: increased data visibility streamlines operations, yet global variability across markets and geographies adds complexity. The goal is to use **Analytics** and **Digital Infrastructure** to manage that complexity effectively.

:::note
Digitization isn't just about collecting data — it's about using that data to handle real-world variabilities across industries like FMCG, E-commerce, and Automotive.
:::

## Course Modules at a Glance

### Module 1 — SCM Fundamentals

Covers the baseline concepts before any digital tools are applied:

- **SC Diversity** — Not all supply chains are the same; structures vary by industry and product
- **SC Drivers** — Key factors determining SC performance
- **Strategic Fit** — Choosing between a **Responsive SC** (speed & flexibility) vs. an **Efficient SC** (low cost)
- **Supply Chain Segmentation** — Managing complexity within a single SC by product, demand pattern, customer type, geography, or channel

### Module 2 — Digital Business Models in SCM

Explores the **Platform Economy** and how it reshapes channel structures:

- Centralized vs. Decentralized decision-making
- Channel evolution: Brick & Mortar → Dual Channel → Multi-channel → **Omni-channel**

### Module 3 — Supply Chain Analytics

From raw data to actionable decisions using the analytics hierarchy:

| Level | Question | Tools |
|---|---|---|
| Descriptive | What happened? | Reporting, dashboards |
| Diagnostic | Why did it happen? | Root cause analysis |
| Predictive | What will happen? | AI/ML, demand forecasting |
| Prescriptive | How to make it happen? | Optimization models |

### Module 4 — Digital Infrastructure & Industry 4.0

Builds the **"Digital Thread"** across the supply chain using core systems (ERP, WMS, TMS) and advanced technologies like **Digital Twins**, **Control Towers**, **IoT**, and **Blockchain**.

## Macro Context: PM GatiShakti National Master Plan (2021)

SCM digitization connects to India's national infrastructure agenda. The **PM GatiShakti plan** aims to:

- Enable **multi-modal logistics and connectivity**
- Break down silos across 16 Ministries
- Solve **last-mile connectivity** challenges

:::tip
SCM digitization is a direct enabler of national efficiency goals — understanding this macro context helps frame why digital transformation in logistics is a strategic priority, not just a technical one.
:::
