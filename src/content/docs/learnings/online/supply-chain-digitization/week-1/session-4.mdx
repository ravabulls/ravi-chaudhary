## 1. SC Processes — Overview

SC processes follow a sequence — the same order in most supply chains.

| # | Process |
|---|---|
| 1 | Planning & Forecasting |
| 2 | Sourcing & Procurement |
| 3 | Production & Manufacturing |
| 4 | Inventory Management |
| 5 | Distribution & Logistics |
| 6 | Customer Service |

Different stakeholders handle different processes; minor variation is possible across industries.

**KPIs** (Key Performance Indicators) are defined for each process to monitor performance and ensure efficient, effective execution.

Sessions 4 and 5 together cover all 6 processes. Session 4 covers Processes 1–3; Session 5 covers Processes 4–6.

## 2. Process 1 — Planning & Forecasting

### Forecasting vs. Planning

<Aside type="note">
**Forecasting:** predicting future demand, future sales, marketing needs, and resource requirements.

**Planning:** deciding how to manage resources (production, inventory, capacity, financials) to fulfil the predicted demand.
</Aside>

**Relationship:** Forecasting → inputs into → Planning (forecasting is done first, planning follows).

### What Planning Covers

- **Make-or-buy decisions:** produce components in-house OR buy from supplier/outsource?
- **Facility role planning:** what qty to manufacture at each plant, store at each warehouse, sell at each retailer
- **SC network structuring:** how nodes (plants, warehouses, retailers) are connected and what role each plays
- **Productivity monitoring:** track performance of all facilities/processes using KPIs

### Forecasting Methods — Worked Example (Simple Moving Average)

**Context:** Online sports equipment store forecasting bicycle sales for August 2023.

**Historical data:**
- May 2023 = 200 units
- June 2023 = 180 units
- July 2023 = 150 units

**Method:** 3-Month Simple Moving Average

**Formula:** Forecast = (Month 1 + Month 2 + Month 3) ÷ 3

**Calculation:** (200 + 180 + 150) ÷ 3 = 530 ÷ 3 ≈ **177 units**

**Value:** the retailer can now plan inventory levels, staffing, and marketing for the holiday season.

<Aside type="note">
**Forecast error** = difference between estimated and actual demand. Measures accuracy using MAD, MSE, RMSE, or Bias.
</Aside>

### KPIs for Forecasting

| KPI | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **MAPE** (Mean Absolute Percentage Error) | Avg % difference between forecasted and actual value — lower is better |
| **MAD** (Mean Absolute Deviation) | Average absolute forecast error |
| **MSE** (Mean Square Error) | Average squared forecast error |
| **RMSE** (Root Mean Square Error) | Square root of MSE |
| **Bias** | Indicates over-forecasting (positive) or under-forecasting (negative) |
| **R²** (R-squared) | Proportion of variation in actual data explained by the model — higher is better (used for ML/regression forecasting) |

## 3. Process 2 — Sourcing & Procurement

### Sourcing

<Aside type="note">
**Definition:** Identifying, evaluating, selecting, and managing suppliers to fulfil company requirements.
</Aside>

**Goals:**
- **Cost reduction:** always the primary goal
- **Diversification:** avoid reliance on a single supplier → reduces risk
- **Quality assurance:** ensure all vendors meet quality standards
- **Innovation support:** suppliers expected to support new product requirements

### Procurement

<Aside type="note">
**Definition:** End-to-end process of acquiring goods, services, or work from external sources — from sourcing all the way to final payment.
</Aside>

**Goals:**
- **Cost control:** across the entire sourcing-to-payment process
- **Timely flow:** track and ensure goods/services flow on time
- **Risk management:** manage supplier relationships to prevent disruption
- **Quality & delivery:** maintain product quality and on-time delivery
- **Compliance:** ensure all procurement activities comply with regulations

<Aside type="note">
**Sourcing vs. Procurement:** Sourcing is a subset of Procurement. Sourcing identifies and selects suppliers → triggers the broader procurement process.
</Aside>

## 4. Process 3 — Production & Manufacturing

Modern production is a **data-rich environment** — new technologies capture point-to-point data and track production progress in real time.

### What Data Analytics Enables in Production

| Application | Description |
|---|---|
| Employee & sales performance tracking | Ensure production goals are met; adjust schedules as needed |
| Predictive maintenance | Predict machine failures before they happen → reduce downtime |
| Quality control (SPC) | Statistical Process Control — monitor and maintain product quality |
| Root cause analysis | ML models identify causes of defects or production issues |
| Process monitoring | Continuous tracking of production parameters |

### Key KPIs for Production & Manufacturing

| KPI | Definition |
|---|---|
| **OEE** (Overall Equipment Effectiveness) | Measures how well equipment is being used: Availability × Performance × Quality |
| **Cycle Time** | Time to complete one unit of production |
| **Throughput** | Number of units produced in a given period |
| **Capacity Utilisation** | % of total capacity actually being used |
