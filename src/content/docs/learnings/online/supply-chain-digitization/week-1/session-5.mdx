## 1. Process 3 (continued) — Production & Manufacturing KPIs

### Three Types of Capacity

<Aside type="note">
**Design Capacity:** maximum capacity the system is available to operate at (ideal/theoretical maximum).

**Effective Capacity:** Design Capacity minus planned unavailability (preventive maintenance, setups, changeovers).

**Actual Capacity (Actual Output):** Effective Capacity minus unplanned unavailability (machine breakdowns, spare part shortages).
</Aside>

### Utilisation & Efficiency — Formulas

| KPI | Formula | Denominator |
|---|---|---|
| **Utilisation** | Actual Output ÷ Design Capacity | Theoretical maximum |
| **Efficiency** | Actual Output ÷ Effective Capacity | Planned usable capacity |

The numerator is the same (Actual Output); only the denominator differs.

### Worked Example — Leather Bag Manufacturer

**Given data:**
- Actual output (last week) = 1,02,000 bags
- Effective capacity = 1,07,500 bags
- Working schedule = 7 days/week × 2 shifts/day × 9 hrs/shift
- Production rate = 900 bags/hour

**Step 1 — Calculate Design Capacity:**
> 7 × 2 × 9 × 900 = **1,13,400 bags**

**Step 2 — Utilisation:**
> 1,02,000 ÷ 1,13,400 = **89.9%**

**Step 3 — Efficiency:**
> 1,02,000 ÷ 1,07,500 = **94.8%**

## 2. Process 4 — Inventory Management

<Aside type="note">
**Definition:** Knowing how much product is available on hand and where it is located → enables SC decision-making.
</Aside>

**Goal:** Balance between adequate stock on hand AND avoiding surplus/hoarding.

### Inventory Management Techniques

| Technique | Description |
|---|---|
| **ABC Analysis** | Classify products into A, B, C categories by importance/value; most widely used |
| **Safety Stock** | Buffer stock based on lead time and lead time variability to cover demand uncertainty |
| **EOQ** (Economic Order Quantity) | Balances ordering cost vs. holding cost to find optimal order quantity |
| **Reorder Point** | Triggers replenishment order to ensure product is always available when needed |

### Key Inventory KPIs

| KPI | Formula |
|---|---|
| **Inventory Turnover Ratio** | COGS ÷ Average Inventory |
| **Inventory Turnover Rate (Days)** | Planning Period ÷ Inventory Turnover Ratio |

### Worked Example — T-Shirt Boutique (Inventory Turnover)

**Planning period:** 1 quarter = 90 days | **COGS:** ₹15,00,000 (same for both scenarios)

| Scenario | Avg Inventory | Turnover Ratio | Rate (Days) | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Low inventory | ₹1,50,000 | 10 | 9 days | More frequent refills → more responsive |
| High inventory | ₹5,00,000 | 3 | 30 days | Less frequent refills → higher holding cost |

<Aside type="note">
Use these KPIs to decide the right inventory level — avoid missing demand while minimising holding cost.
</Aside>

## 3. Process 5 — Distribution & Logistics

**Role:** Ensure product is available to customers as per their requirement; coordinate order management → warehousing → inventory management → transportation.

**Customer expectation:** real-time order visibility and ability to track shipment.

### Role of Data Analytics in Distribution & Logistics

| Application | Description |
|---|---|
| Route optimisation | Analyse historical traffic and weather data → optimise delivery routes in real time |
| Warehouse optimisation | Space utilisation, product placement, picking process efficiency |
| Fleet optimisation | Vehicle routing, fuel consumption, maintenance scheduling |
| Inventory optimisation | Right stock at right location |
| Real-time visibility | Track orders through the distribution network |

### Distribution & Logistics KPIs

| KPI | Definition |
|---|---|
| Transportation Cost per Unit | Cost to move one unit |
| Order Cycle Time | Time from order placement to fulfilment |
| **OTD** (On-Time Delivery) | % of orders delivered on or before promised date |
| Inventory Turnover Rate | How quickly inventory is sold through |
| **OTIF** (On-Time In-Full) | Delivered on time AND in correct quantity |

### Worked Example — 3PL Selection (Total Cost Analysis)

**Context:** Company choosing between Avitruck and Logitruck for 3 product types (Modern, Semi-Modern, Classical).

**Total Cost = Annual Inventory Carrying Cost + Annual Transportation Cost**

| Term | Formula |
|---|---|
| Cycle Stock | 0.5 × Shipment Size |
| Pipeline Inventory | Lead Time × Demand Rate |
| Total Inventory | Cycle Stock + Pipeline Inventory |
| Annual Carrying Cost | Total Inventory × Unit Cost × 25% |
| Annual Transport Cost | Annual Demand (weekly × 52) × Transport Cost/unit |

**Sample calculation (Logitruck + Modern product):**
- Shipment size = 350 → Cycle Stock = 0.5 × 350 = **175 units**
- Lead time = 3 wks, Demand = 150 units/wk → Pipeline Inv. = 3 × 150 = **450 units**
- Total Inventory = 175 + 450 = **625 units**
- Annual inventory carrying cost = 625 × 18,000 × 0.25 = **₹28,12,500**
- Transport Cost = 150 × 52 × ₹100 = **₹7,80,000/year**
- **Total Cost ≈ ₹35,92,500**

**Result (all combinations analysed):**

| Product | Best Provider |
|---|---|
| Modern | Avitruck (lower total cost) |
| Semi-Modern | Logitruck (lower total cost) |
| Classical | Logitruck (lower total cost) |

## 4. Process 6 — Customer Service

**Role:** Drives the whole SC; focuses on providing support to customers throughout their experience.

### 3 Core Customer Expectations

| Expectation | Description |
|---|---|
| Effective communication | Real-time order tracking and visibility |
| Responsiveness | Fast responses to queries and issues |
| Quality | Consistently high product and service quality |

### Scope of Digitisation in Customer Service

- Improved inventory management → faster response times → more customers attracted
- Order tracking → transparency and visibility for customers
- Reverse logistics: returns, refunds, exchanges managed efficiently via digitised systems

### Customer Service KPIs

| KPI | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Order Fulfilment Cycle Time** | Time from order placement to delivery |
| **OTD** (On-Time Delivery) | % of orders delivered on or before promised date |
| **Fill Rate** | % of customer demand fulfilled immediately from stock — no backorders |
| **Return Rate** | % of products returned; critical in e-commerce |
| **Supplier On-Time Delivery Rate** | % of times supplier delivers RM on time |
| **Compliance with SLAs** | Adherence to agreed service level agreements |

### Worked Example — Soap Bar Manufacturer

| KPI | Formula | Result | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| On-Time Delivery | (720 ÷ 800) × 100 | **90%** | 90% of orders delivered on time → excellent |
| Delivery Cost per Soap Bar | ₹400 ÷ 720 | **₹0.56/bar** | Shipping fee per unit; useful for comparing logistics options |
| Supplier On-Time Delivery Rate | 75 ÷ 80 | **93.75%** | Suppliers deliver RM on time 93.75% of the time → good |

## 5. Module 1 — Full Summary (All 6 SC Processes)

| Process | Purpose | Key KPIs |
|---|---|---|
| Planning & Forecasting | Predict demand; plan resources | MAPE, Bias, R² |
| Sourcing & Procurement | Acquire goods/services efficiently | Cost, compliance, delivery |
| Production & Manufacturing | Convert RM to FG | OEE, Utilisation, Efficiency, Throughput |
| Inventory Management | Balance stock with demand | Turnover Ratio, Turnover Rate |
| Distribution & Logistics | Move product to customer | OTD, OTIF, Transport Cost/Unit |
| Customer Service | Support throughout experience | Fill Rate, Return Rate, Order Cycle Time |
