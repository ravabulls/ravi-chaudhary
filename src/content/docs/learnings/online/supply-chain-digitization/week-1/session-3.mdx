## Quick Recap (from Session 2)

- Competitive Strategy → Competitive Priority → Manufacturing Process → SC Strategy
- **Cost focus:** high volume, low variety → Assembly / Continuous process
- **Differentiation focus:** high variety, lower volume → Job Shop process
- **SC strategy:** Responsive (speed, fulfil customer need fast) OR Efficient (minimise cost)

---

## 1. SC Drivers — Overview

<Aside type="note">
**Definition:** Levers a SC uses to balance responsiveness vs. efficiency, and maximise overall SC profit.
</Aside>

**6 Drivers:** Facilities, Inventory, Transportation *(Logistical)* + Information, Sourcing, Pricing *(Cross-sectional)*

| Category | Drivers |
|---|---|
| Logistical | Facilities, Inventory, Transportation — physical entities |
| Cross-sectional | Information, Sourcing, Pricing — enable and coordinate the physical drivers |

---

## 2. Driver 1 — Facilities

Includes: plant, warehouse, retail store — places where inventory is stored or converted.

**Role:** receive, store, or convert RM / WIP / FG at each node of the SC.

### Three Facility Decisions

**(a) Capacity**

| Option | Effect | SC Type |
|---|---|---|
| Large capacity | Handles higher volumes, lower unit cost | Efficient |
| Small capacity | Adaptable to demand/market changes | Responsive |

**(b) Location**

| Option | Effect | SC Type |
|---|---|---|
| Centralised | Economies of scale | Efficient |
| Decentralised (closer to customers) | Faster availability | Responsive |

**(c) Production Facility Type**

| Type | Feature | SC Type |
|---|---|---|
| Flexible (multi-product, shared equipment) | Higher variety | Responsive |
| Dedicated (fixed machines, limited variety) | Large qty, low variety | Efficient |

**(d) Warehouse Type**

| Type | Feature | SC Type |
|---|---|---|
| Cross-docking | Inventory moves without long storage, mix-and-match per customer need | Responsive |
| Dedicated storage | Large volumes, minimal cost | Efficient |

---

## 3. Driver 2 — Inventory

**Primary role:** bridge the gap between supply and demand.

### 3 Components of Inventory

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| **Cycle inventory** | Same qty ordered repeatedly in a cyclic pattern |
| **Safety inventory** | Minimum buffer stock to prevent stockouts/process disruption |
| **Seasonal inventory** | Extra stock carried during peak/seasonal demand periods |

### Trade-off

| Approach | Upside | Downside |
|---|---|---|
| High inventory | Fulfils demand fully → Responsive | Adds holding cost |
| Low inventory | Lowers holding cost | Poor service level, lost customers |

### Inventory Location Strategy

| SC Type | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Responsive | Carry high FG inventory, place it closer to consumers |
| Efficient | Carry FG at centralised location → order consolidation + transport optimisation |

<Aside type="note">
A SC can be a mix of responsive and efficient — it is not always a pure strategy.
</Aside>

---

## 4. Driver 3 — Transportation

Moves RM: supplier → manufacturer AND FG: manufacturer → final customer.

### (a) Network Design

| Option | Description |
|---|---|
| Direct shipping | Manufacturer → demand point directly (no intermediary) |
| Through intermediaries | Depends on whether SC needs to be responsive or efficient |

### (b) Mode of Transport

| Mode | Speed | Cost | SC Type | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Air | Fast | High | Responsive | High-value, low-demand products |
| Sea | Slow | Low | Efficient | Low-value, high-demand products |
| Rail / Road | Intermediate | Intermediate | Both | — |

---

## 5. Driver 4 — Information

Enables and coordinates all other 5 drivers; critical for both push and pull systems.

### Push vs. Pull

| Strategy | Trigger | Information Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Push** | Forecast / historical demand | Advance planning |
| **Pull** | Actual customer demand/order | Quick response and partner collaboration |

### Information by SC Type

| SC Type | Information Used For |
|---|---|
| Efficient | Forecasting, inventory mgmt, supplier collaboration, production planning, transport optimisation |
| Responsive | Real-time visibility (ML), demand sensing, demand shaping via promotions, scenario planning |

---

## 6. Driver 5 — Sourcing

### 3 Components

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| Procurement | Decisions on types of materials required (see Kraljic Matrix in Week 2) |
| Sourcing strategy | Single vs. multiple sourcing; in-house vs. outsourcing |
| Supplier selection | Identify requirements → find suppliers who can fulfil them |

### Sourcing by SC Type

| SC Type | Approach |
|---|---|
| Efficient | Cost optimisation + economies of scale + supplier consolidation |
| Responsive | Supplier collaboration (handle disruptions) + maintain alternative suppliers + customisation strategies |

---

## 7. Driver 6 — Pricing

| Pricing Strategy | SC Type |
|---|---|
| Economies of scale pricing (big batch) | Efficient |
| EDLP (Everyday Low Pricing) | Efficient |
| High-Low Pricing (discounts at different times) | Responsive |
| Fixed Pricing | Efficient |
| Menu Pricing (varies by response time, location, attributes) | Responsive |

<Aside type="note">
Customers wanting responsiveness vs. efficiency are entirely different segments — the right pricing strategy targets the right one.
</Aside>

---

## Summary: All 6 Drivers

| Driver | Responsive SC | Efficient SC |
|---|---|---|
| Facilities | Decentralised, flexible, cross-docking | Centralised, dedicated, large capacity |
| Inventory | High FG near consumer | Low, centralised |
| Transportation | Air, rapid, direct | Sea/rail/road, low-cost |
| Information | Real-time visibility, demand sensing | Forecasting, planning, optimisation |
| Sourcing | Collaboration, alternate suppliers | Cost optimisation, consolidation |
| Pricing | High-Low, Menu | EDLP, Fixed, Economies of scale |

<Aside type="tip">
**Exam angle:** "Explain the role of SC drivers in making a SC responsive or efficient." → Define responsive vs. efficient → name all 6 drivers with categories → use the table above to explain each driver's role for both strategies.
</Aside>
