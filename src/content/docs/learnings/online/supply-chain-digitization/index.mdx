## The Shift to Digital SCM

SCM is evolving due to high data availability (operations, purchases, payments) and the rise of the **Platform Economy**. The core tension is:

- **Data visibility** → Streamlined operations
- **Global variability** (markets, geography) → Increased complexity

**Goal:** Use Analytics and Digital Infrastructure to manage this complexity across industries like FMCG, E-commerce, and Automotive.

<Aside type="note">
Digitization isn't just about data — it's about using that data to handle global supply chain variabilities.
</Aside>

## Module 1: SCM Fundamentals

**Key Idea:** Understanding Supply Chain Diversity & Drivers

- Not all supply chains are the same — structures vary by industry and product
- **SC Drivers:** key factors that determine SC performance
- **Strategic Fit:** choosing between a **Responsive SC** (speed/flexibility) vs. **Efficient SC** (low cost)

### Supply Chain Segmentation

Why segment? To manage specific challenges within a single SC.

**Methods of segmentation:**
- Product-based
- Demand patterns
- Customer type
- Geography
- Channel (e.g., online vs. offline)

<Aside type="note">
Fundamental understanding of drivers and segmentation is the baseline before applying digital tools.
</Aside>

## Module 2: Digital Business Models in SCM

**Key Idea:** The Platform Economy & Channel Structures

- **Platform Economy:** foundations seen in taxi aggregators, e-commerce, and ticketing
- **Channel Structures:** Centralized vs. Decentralized — how decisions and inventory are controlled
- **Evolution of Channels:** Brick & Mortar → Dual Channel → Multi-channel → Omni-channel (seamless integration)

<Aside type="note">
Digital business requires moving beyond traditional brick-and-mortar to integrated Omni-channel structures.
</Aside>

## Module 3: Supply Chain Analytics

**Key Idea:** From Data to Decisions

**Big Data in SCM:** high-volume, high-velocity data specific to logistics.

### The Analytics Hierarchy

| Level | Question | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| Descriptive | What happened? | Reporting, dashboards |
| Diagnostic | Why did it happen? | Root cause analysis |
| Predictive | What will happen? | AI/ML, demand forecasting |
| Prescriptive | How to make it happen? | Optimization models |

### Network Optimization (Prescriptive)

**Objective:** Minimize total cost while improving service levels.

**Key decisions:**
- **Facility Location:** where to place Plants, Warehouses (WH), Distribution Centers (DC)
- **Allocation:** mapping which plant serves which WH/DC
- **Product Flow:** movement of goods across the network

<Aside type="note">
AI/ML and Python-based tools are the modern toolset for demand forecasting and cost-minimizing network design.
</Aside>

## Module 4: Digital Infrastructure & Industry 4.0

**Key Idea:** Building the "Digital Thread"

**Tracking & Traceability** creates a trail of:
- Material flow
- Financial transactions
- Information resources

### Core Information Systems

| System | Function |
|---|---|
| ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) | Internal integration |
| WMS (Warehouse Management System) | Inventory and space control |
| TMS (Transport Management System) | Logistics and freight |

### Advanced Tech: Digital Twins & Control Towers

**Problem:** Geographically dispersed SCs lack end-to-end (E2E) visibility.

**Solution:**
- **Digital Twin:** a virtual replica of the SC to test scenarios
- **Control Tower:** a central hub for real-time monitoring and control
- **Industry 4.0:** integration of IoT, Blockchain (secure transactions), and Digital Payments

<Aside type="note">
Digital twins and control towers provide the E2E visibility needed to manage global, fragmented supply chains.
</Aside>

**Objective:** Facilitate multi-modal logistics and connectivity across India.

- **The Silo Problem:** integrating 16 Ministries to break departmental barriers

- **Relevance to SCM:** aligns with national goals for operational efficiency and accelerated development

SCM digitization is a key enabler for national initiatives like PM GatiShakti, focusing on multimodal efficiency.
