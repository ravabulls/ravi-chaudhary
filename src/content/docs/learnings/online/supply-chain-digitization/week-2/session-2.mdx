## 8 Needs for Segmentation

### Need 1: Diverse Customer Needs

Customers have different needs, preferences, and expectations → SC must be designed accordingly.

| Customer Type | Requirements | SC Design Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Retail customers | Personalized services (home delivery, promotions, e-commerce) | Individual consumer requirements |
| B2B clients | Bulk shipments, streamlined procurement | Cost reduction in large-scale handling |

**Strategy:** Understand customer type → design SC around customized services, faster delivery, or better order fulfilment.

### Need 2: Varying Demand Patterns

Demand is never constant — varies by market, region, and customer group.

| Region | Expectation | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| High Demand | On-time fulfilment always | Large inventory + fast replenishment cycles |
| Low Demand | Avoid excess cost | Smaller stock levels + JIT replenishment |

<Aside type="note">
**Understanding demand = controlling the entire SC cascade:**
Demand characteristics → Inventory levels → Production schedules → Distribution strategy.
</Aside>

### Need 3: Product Characteristics

Companies offer a variety of products — each needs a different SC approach.

**Key characteristics that drive segmentation:**
- Product life cycle stage (new vs. established)
- Demand volatility
- Special handling requirements
- Perishability vs. durability

| Product Type | SC Approach |
|---|---|
| **Perishable** (short shelf life) | Short/fast SC, expedited shipping, real-time tracking |
| **Durable** (long shelf life) | Cost-effective storage and transport; efficiency-focused |

### Need 4: Operational Efficiency

Traditional SC is linear and cost-focused — cannot handle unique customer requirements.

**Solution: Segment products as Standard vs. Customized**

| Product Type | Production | SC Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard** (e.g., salt) | Continuous, systematic | Reduced lead time + minimized waste |
| **Customized** | Dedicated lines + flexible scheduling | Faster response to customer-specific needs |

### Need 5: Cost Reduction

Primary objective of all SC = manage and reduce cost.

| Category | Approach |
|---|---|
| **Standard products** | Standard/bulk packaging + cost-efficient logistics |
| **High-value products** | Specialized packaging + premium shipping; prioritize customer satisfaction over capacity utilisation |

<Aside type="note">
Knowing the product category → allocate right resources → avoid over-spending or under-serving.
</Aside>

### Need 6: Risk Management

SC is global and complex → exposed to many types of risk. Even one partner failing affects the whole SC.

| Component Type | Risk Level | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** (e.g., semiconductors) | High | Dual sourcing, safety stock, close supplier collaboration |
| **Non-critical** | Lower | Single sourcing acceptable; flexible arrangements |

<Aside type="note">
Criticality of component determines sourcing and risk mitigation strategy.
</Aside>

### Need 7: Customer-Centric Approach

Customers expect personalized experiences and services.

| Customer Tier | Service Offered |
|---|---|
| Premium customers | Exclusive deals + faster shipping options |
| Standard customers | Standard services + standard shipping |

**Goal:** Ensure customer loyalty and positive brand perception.

### Need 8: Adapting to Market Dynamics

Economic conditions and regulatory changes directly impact SC requirements.

| Market Type | Characteristics | SC Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Emerging Markets** | Low income, dense population, poor infrastructure, political/cultural marginalization | Agile SC — manage demand fluctuations rapidly |
| **Mature Markets** | Stable, predictable demand | Cost efficiency; standard efficient SC |

## 7 Types of Supply Chain Segmentation

### 1. Customer Segmentation
Segment retail customers, wholesale customers, B2B clients, etc. Design SC strategy based on each customer group's requirements.

### 2. Product-Based Segmentation
Key characteristics used:
- Demand volatility
- Shelf life / Perishability
- Specific handling requirements
- Product types: High-value / Fast-moving / Slow-moving / Perishable

### 3. Geographic Segmentation
Based on: region, demand pattern, regulatory requirements, distribution network needs. Helps design tailored SC strategies region by region.

### 4. Channel Segmentation
Channels: Direct sales / Retailers / E-commerce. Each channel has different practices and delivery requirements — SC must be tailored per channel.

### 5. Value Chain Segmentation
Focus on value-added activities at each stage of SC. Different strategies for: Suppliers / Manufacturers / Distributors. Goal: Optimize each function within the value chain.

### 6. Demand-Based Segmentation
Based on seasonality or variability of demand.
- Seasonal demand → different SC strategy
- Steady demand → managed with efficient, consistent SC

### 7. Inventory-Based Segmentation
Classify inventories using:
- ABC Analysis
- XYZ Analysis
- Lead Time Analysis

Segment stored products based on inventory characteristics.

## Key Takeaways

- Segmentation = breaking large SC problem into smaller manageable segments
- No single segmentation type works alone — always a **combination** of types is needed
- Different segments require different strategies — not one-size-fits-all
- Segmentation connects directly to managing every SC challenge from Session 1

<Aside type="tip">
**Exam tip:** If asked "why segment?", use the 8 needs framework. If asked "how to segment?", list all 7 types with one distinguishing feature each.

Segmentation → tailored strategies → reduced cost, better service, managed risk.
</Aside>
