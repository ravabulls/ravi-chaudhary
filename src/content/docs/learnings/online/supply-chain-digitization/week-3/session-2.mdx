## DuPont Model — Adapted for Operations

<Aside type="note">
**Central metric:** Return on Assets (ROA)

**Core formula:**

**ROA = Net Profit Margin × Asset Turnover Ratio**

Both are *derived indicators* — calculated from other metrics, not directly measured.
</Aside>

**Goal:** Maximize both components to maximize ROA.

---

## Part 1: Net Profit Margin

<Aside type="note">
**Net Profit Margin = Net Profit / Sales**

How much profit is earned as a proportion of sales. Can range from <1% to >100% — higher is better.
</Aside>

### Sales

> **Sales = Price per unit × Volume (units sold)**

### Net Profit

> **Net Profit = Sales − COGS − SGA − Interest Expenses − Taxes − ...**

### Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

All costs **directly** related to producing and distributing the product:
- Cost of purchase: amount paid to vendors for raw materials
- Labour cost: workers in manufacturing (packaging, cleaning, equipment setup, material movement)

### SGA Expenses (Selling, General & Administrative)

Costs **not directly** linked to manufacturing:
- **Selling:** advertisement and promotion costs
- **General & Administrative:** IT staff, infrastructure management, customer relations, non-shop-floor employees

### Other Deductions

- **Interest expenses:** cost of borrowing from banks
- **Taxes:** income tax payable to government

<Aside type="note">
The firm owes money to: vendors (COGS), employees (COGS + SGA), bank (interest), government (tax). All reduce net profit.
</Aside>

### Operations Levers for Net Profit Margin

| Lever | Effect |
|---|---|
| Reduce cost of inputs (raw material procurement) | Lower COGS |
| Reduce labour cost | Lower COGS |
| Reduce advertising / distribution costs | Lower SGA |
| Choose tax-advantaged locations (e.g., manufacturing in an SEZ) | Lower tax liability |
| Increase sales volume / price | Higher revenue base |

## Part 2: Asset Turnover Ratio

<Aside type="note">
**Asset Turnover Ratio = Sales / Total Assets**

How much sales are generated per unit of assets owned. Higher = better — means assets are being utilized efficiently.
</Aside>

### Total Assets = Current Assets + Fixed Assets

#### Fixed Assets
- Physical assets used to produce/deliver the product — not sold as product
- Examples: Manufacturing plants, factories, equipment, fleet of vehicles
- Part of capital expenditure — expensive to buy AND sell
- Not easily liquidated — take time to purchase and sell

#### Current Assets
- Assets that can be quickly liquidated (typically within the year)

| Current Asset | Description |
|---|---|
| **Inventory** | Finished goods, WIP, raw materials — can be sold → generates cash |
| **Receivables** | Money owed to firm by customers who purchased on credit (common in B2B) |
| **Cash & Other Current Assets** | Already liquid — used for wages, small vendors, day-to-day expenses |

<Aside type="note">
Current assets = keep operations running day-to-day. Fixed assets = enable production and revenue generation. Both are needed — but excess of either is inefficient.
</Aside>

### Operations Levers for Asset Turnover Ratio

| Lever | Effect |
|---|---|
| Reduce excess inventory | Lower current assets, better turnover |
| Increase inventory turnover — move stock out quickly | Lower average inventory |
| Improve cash-to-cash cycle speed | Faster cash regeneration from operations |
| Improve asset utilization — maximize equipment/machinery use | Better output per unit of fixed asset |
| Outsource if assets are expensive | Reduce fixed asset ownership |

---

## Critical Insight: Levers Are Interconnected

Improving one lever impacts other levers — cannot optimize in isolation.

| Example Action | Trade-off |
|---|---|
| Increase advertising (higher SGA) | May increase sales — net effect on profit margin unclear |
| Reduce inventory too aggressively | → Stockouts → lower sales |
| Outsource to 3PL | Reduces fixed assets but increases operational expenses (3PL fees) |

<Aside type="caution">
Design strategy by looking at multiple financial indicators together — not one in isolation. ROA is just one indicator; others matter too: quick ratio, current ratio, ROCE, inventory days, etc.
</Aside>

## Industry Examples — Operations Strategy Linked to Financial Performance

### Example 1: Electric Vehicles for Last-Mile Delivery

- **Context:** Same-day / 10-minute delivery → high operational cost (fuel, manpower, multiple trips). In India, ~50%+ of logistics cost = transportation cost (mainly fuel-dependent road transport).
- **Solution:** Electric Vehicles (EV) for last-mile delivery; EV charged via renewable energy → not dependent on conventional fuel
- **DuPont Impact:** Reduces COGS / operating cost → **improves Net Profit Margin → improves ROA**
- **Seen in:** Food delivery, grocery delivery sectors

### Example 2: Asset-Light Model via 3PL (Third-Party Logistics)

- **Context:** High logistics cost → difficult for individual firm to own and manage a full fleet
- **Solution:** Outsource to 3PL providers who aggregate volumes from multiple clients → economies of scale → pass cost savings to clients
- **DuPont Impact:** Firm removes fixed assets (no owned vehicles) → **improves Asset Turnover Ratio → improves ROA**
- This is the **asset-light model** — using assets without owning them

### Example 3: Digital Transformation — Automating Information Processing

- **Context:** Manual order processing, documentation, email management → wastes time and increases admin cost
- **Solutions:** Email automation; e-commerce order management systems → sellers and buyers negotiate without manual intervention
- **DuPont Impact:** Reduces SGA / admin expenses → **improves Net Profit Margin**

### Example 4: UX-Driven Inventory Turnover in E-Commerce

- **Context:** E-commerce platforms need to move inventory out quickly from warehouses/fulfilment centers
- **Strategy:** Clever website/app design → better navigation → consumer selects → pays → exits quickly or buys more
- **DuPont Impact:** Higher sales + faster inventory liquidation → **improves Asset Turnover Ratio**
- Also reduces amount of inventory held in the system at any given time
- Shows: even **digital design decisions** have direct financial performance implications

## Session Summary

| Component | Formula | Improve By |
|---|---|---|
| **Net Profit Margin** | Net Profit / Sales | Cut input/labour/admin/ad costs; choose tax-advantaged locations |
| **Asset Turnover Ratio** | Sales / Total Assets | Reduce inventory; improve utilization; outsource fixed assets |
| **ROA** | Net Profit Margin × Asset Turnover Ratio | Improve both components above |

| Industry Example | DuPont Impact |
|---|---|
| EVs for last-mile | Reduces COGS → better Net Profit Margin |
| 3PL outsourcing | Removes fixed assets → better Asset Turnover |
| Digitizing admin processes | Reduces SGA → better Net Profit Margin |
| UX design in e-commerce | Boosts inventory turnover → better Asset Turnover |

<Aside type="tip">
**Exam tip:** If asked how operations strategy connects to financial performance — use the DuPont framework. Trace any SC decision (outsourcing, lean inventory, EV adoption) through to its impact on Net Profit Margin or Asset Turnover Ratio → then to ROA.
</Aside>
