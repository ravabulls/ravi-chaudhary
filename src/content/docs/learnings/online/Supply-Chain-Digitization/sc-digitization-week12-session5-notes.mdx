## 1. Blockchain — Definition & Role

**Definition:** a distributed ledger technology where secure and transparent recording of transactions and data occurs across a network of computers.

Two defining properties — **both simultaneously**:
- **Secure:** tamper-proof, cryptographically protected.
- **Transparent:** visible to all authorized network members.

How it works: transactions are gathered into blocks and recorded as a **chronological chain** — hence 'blockchain'.

In Industry 4.0: blockchain is an enabler of digitalization — critical for SC transparency and traceability.

### Blockchain in SC — What it Enables
- Track suppliers, vendors, material sources, and pricing across the SC.
- Secure, tamper-proof record keeping of all transactions and product movements.
- Builds **trust** between SC partners — no single party can alter the shared history.
- Applications: finance, supply chain, healthcare, voting, and any domain with multi-party transactions.

<Aside type="note">
**★** Blockchain is NOT just a database. It is a **distributed, tamper-evident record that no single party controls** — this makes it uniquely suited for multi-partner SC transactions.
</Aside>

## 2. How Blockchain Works — 3-Step Process

<Steps>
1. **Transaction Recording:** When a transaction occurs, all details are captured as a **block of data**. Details recorded: who did it, when, where, for what purpose, how much — full transaction metadata. The block = a complete, structured record of one transaction.
2. **Blockchain Formation:** Multiple blocks are linked together in chronological order → forming a chain. Before a new block is added, it must be **VALIDATED** by the network via a **consensus mechanism**. Consensus ensures no single party can add fraudulent data — all network members must agree.
3. **Immutability:** Each new block also **re-verifies the previous block** when being added → strengthens the chain. Result: data in the blockchain is tamper-evident — any change is instantly detectable. Immutable = cannot be altered retroactively by any malicious actor → trusted ledger for all members.
</Steps>

**Consensus mechanism:** before any block is added, all network members must agree it is valid — prevents any single fraudulent entry.

**Cryptographic linking:** each block contains a cryptographic reference to the previous block → changing one block would break the chain → instantly detectable.

<Aside type="note">
**★** Transaction → Block → Validation (consensus) → Added to Chain → Immutable. This 3-step cycle builds trust **without a central authority**.
</Aside>

## 3. Types of Blockchain Networks

| Network Type | Who Controls / Joins | Speed | Security | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Public** | Anyone — open access, fully decentralized | Slower | More secure (decentralized) | Cryptocurrency, decentralized apps |
| **Private** | Single org governs and controls who participates | Faster | High (limited access) | Internal process streamlining, efficiency improvement |
| **Permissioned** | Participants need approval from a governing body to join | Fast | High | Where high control over network membership is required |
| **Consortium** | Group of pre-selected orgs jointly govern the network | Fast | High | Inter-org collaboration on a shared project, no single governing body |
| **Hybrid** | Combination of two or more of the above | Varies | Varies | Complex enterprise environments needing flexibility |

### Selection Logic

| Need | Network Type |
|---|---|
| Openness + decentralization | **Public** (e.g., cryptocurrency) |
| Single org, internal processes | **Private** (faster, more controlled) |
| Membership approval required | **Permissioned** |
| Multiple orgs sharing a project | **Consortium** (no single governing org) |
| Complex mix of requirements | **Hybrid** |

## 5. IoT — Case Studies Across 5 SC Functions

*Note: all company names are hypothetical — cases developed for learning purposes.*

| SC Function | Company & Industry | Challenge → IoT Solution → Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Planning & Forecasting** | **Omega Foods** *(Grocery Distributor)* | **Challenge:** poor demand forecasting for perishables → spoilage or backorders. **IoT solution:** smart sensors tracking temperature + stock levels across warehouses and delivery trucks in real time. **Outcome:** predict consumption rate + shelf life → adjust delivery schedules → targeted promotions → reduced spoilage. |
| **Sourcing & Procurement** | **Acme Pharmaceuticals** *(Drug Manufacturer)* | **Challenge:** frequent stockouts and delays due to manual inventory tracking. **IoT solution:** smart sensors in shelves and material racks → real-time stock data → auto-trigger purchase orders below threshold. **Outcome:** stockouts eliminated, procurement cycle optimized via smart contracts, overall cost reduced. |
| **Production & Manufacturing** | **Newton Instruments** *(Violin Maker)* | **Challenge:** maintaining optimal humidity critical for violin quality — fluctuations cause cracks and warping → waste and delays. **IoT solution:** humidity sensors in workshop → smart vents auto-adjust airflow to maintain consistent environment. **Outcome:** consistently controlled environment → increased yield → waste reduced → quality preserved. |
| **Inventory Management** | **Vendor Guitars** *(Instrument Maker)* | **Challenge:** instruments lost during shipping → impacts customer service and operational efficiency. **IoT solution:** location tracking sensors embedded in guitar cases → real-time visibility throughout entire SC. **Outcome:** lost instruments eliminated, deliveries expedited, logistics optimized, customer satisfaction improved. |
| **Distribution & Logistics** | **Best Pharmaceuticals** *(Drug Distributor)* | **Challenge:** spoilage of temperature-sensitive medications due to inconsistent cold chain conditions. **IoT solution:** sensors in shipping containers track real-time temperature and humidity throughout transit. **Outcome:** complete shipment condition visibility → routes and storage optimized → medication quality preserved. |

### Cross-Case Observations

- **Common IoT pattern:** real-time sensor data → analytics engine → automated action or optimized decision.
- **IoT benefits seen across cases:** reduced waste, eliminated stockouts, improved customer service, quality preservation, cost reduction.
- In all 5 cases, the IoT implementation either **prevents a problem** (proactive) or **enables faster recovery** (reactive).

<Aside type="note">
**★** IoT in SC = sense → transmit → analyze → act. Each case study demonstrates a different 'sense' objective: temperature, stock level, humidity, location, or shipment condition.
</Aside>
