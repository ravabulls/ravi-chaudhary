## SC Drivers — Overview

<Aside type="note">
**Definition:** Levers a supply chain uses to balance **responsiveness** vs. **efficiency**, and maximise overall SC profit.

There are **6 SC Drivers**, grouped into two categories:
- **Logistical Drivers** — physical entities: Facilities, Inventory, Transportation
- **Cross-sectional Drivers** — enable and coordinate the physical drivers: Information, Sourcing, Pricing
</Aside>

## Driver 1 — Facilities

<Badge text="Logistical Driver" variant="note" />

Facilities include plants, warehouses, and retail stores — any physical place where inventory is stored or converted. Their role is to receive, store, or convert raw materials, WIP, or finished goods at each node of the supply chain.

There are four facility decision dimensions, each with a Responsive and an Efficient option:

### (a) Capacity

| Decision | SC Impact |
|---|---|
| **Large capacity** | Handles higher volumes, fewer resources needed, lower unit cost → **Efficient SC** |
| **Small capacity** | Adaptable to demand and market changes, higher unit cost → **Responsive SC** |

### (b) Location

| Decision | SC Impact |
|---|---|
| **Centralised** | Economies of scale → **Efficient SC** |
| **Decentralised** (closer to customers) | Faster availability → **Responsive SC** |

### (c) Production Facility Type

| Decision | SC Impact |
|---|---|
| **Flexible** (multi-product, shared equipment and workers) | Higher variety → **Responsive SC** |
| **Dedicated** (fixed machines and manpower, limited variety) | Large quantity, low variety → **Efficient SC** |

### (d) Warehouse Type

| Decision | SC Impact |
|---|---|
| **Cross-docking** | Inventory moves without long storage; mix-and-match per customer need → **Responsive SC** |
| **Dedicated storage** | Large volumes, minimal cost → **Efficient SC** |

## Driver 2 — Inventory

<Badge text="Logistical Driver" variant="note" />

<Aside type="note">
**Primary role of inventory:** Bridge the gap between supply and demand.
</Aside>

### 3 Components of Inventory

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| **Cycle Inventory** | The same quantity ordered repeatedly in a cyclic pattern |
| **Safety Inventory** | Minimum buffer stock maintained to prevent stockouts or process disruption |
| **Seasonal Inventory** | Extra stock carried during peak or seasonal demand periods |

### The Inventory Trade-off

| Inventory Level | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **High inventory** | Fulfils demand fully → **Responsive SC** — but adds holding cost |
| **Low inventory** | Lowers holding cost → **Efficient SC** — but risks poor service levels and lost customers (severe business penalty) |

### Inventory Location Strategy

| SC Type | Strategy |
|---|---|
| **Responsive** | Carry high finished goods inventory; place it **closer to consumers** for faster availability |
| **Efficient** | Carry finished goods at a **centralised location** → enables order consolidation and transportation optimisation → lower cost |

<Aside type="tip">
**Application of Concepts:** A supply chain can be a **mix** of responsive and efficient — it does not have to be a pure strategy. For example, a company may hold centralised safety stock (efficient) while keeping a small responsive buffer near key urban markets.
</Aside>

## Driver 3 — Transportation

<Badge text="Logistical Driver" variant="note" />

Transportation moves raw materials from supplier to manufacturer, and finished goods from manufacturer to the final customer. Two key decisions govern transportation strategy:

### (a) Network Design

| Design | Description |
|---|---|
| **Direct shipping** | Manufacturer ships straight to the demand point with no intermediary |
| **Through intermediaries** | Routed via distributors or hubs depending on whether the SC needs to be responsive or efficient |

### (b) Mode of Transport

| Mode | Speed | Cost | SC Type | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Air** | Fast | High | Responsive | High-value, low-demand products |
| **Sea** | Slow | Low | Efficient | Low-value, high-demand products |
| **Rail / Road** | Intermediate | Intermediate | Mixed | Balance of cost and speed |

### Transport Strategy Summary

| SC Type | Approach |
|---|---|
| **Responsive** | Rapid transport modes (Air); centralised facility stores wide variety; direct delivery to consumer |
| **Efficient** | Low-cost transport modes (Sea/Rail); bring inventory closer to customers to minimise last-mile cost |

## Driver 4 — Information

<Badge text="Cross-Sectional Driver" variant="caution" />

<Aside type="note">
Information is the master driver — it enables and coordinates all five other drivers. It is critical for both push and pull production systems.
</Aside>

### Push vs. Pull Systems

| System | How it works | Role of Information |
|---|---|---|
| **Push** | Production based on forecast and historical demand; products made or services planned in advance of actual orders | Used for advance planning and scheduling |
| **Pull** | Production triggered by actual customer demand or order | Enables quick response and real-time partner collaboration |

### Other Roles of Information

- **SC coordination:** Every SC phase collaborates to maximise overall SC profitability through information sharing across partners.
- **Cross-functional alignment:** Sales, marketing, and production are all aligned to business requirements via shared information systems.

### Information Strategy by SC Type

| SC Type | Information Applications |
|---|---|
| **Efficient SC** | Forecasting, inventory management, supplier collaboration, production planning, transport optimisation |
| **Responsive SC** | Real-time visibility (ML methods), demand sensing, demand shaping via promotions, scenario planning — all aimed at reducing response time |

## Driver 5 — Sourcing

<Badge text="Cross-Sectional Driver" variant="caution" />

### 3 Components of Sourcing Decisions

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| **Procurement** | Decisions on the types of materials required (covered in detail via the Kraljic Matrix in future sessions) |
| **Sourcing strategy** | Single vs. multiple sourcing \| In-house production vs. outsourcing |
| **Supplier selection** | Identify requirements → find and qualify suppliers who can fulfil them |

### Sourcing Strategy by SC Type

| SC Type | Sourcing Approach |
|---|---|
| **Efficient SC** | Cost optimisation + economies of scale + supplier consolidation |
| **Responsive SC** | Supplier collaboration to handle disruptions quickly + maintain a base of **alternative suppliers** so no demand goes unfulfilled + customisation strategies |

<Aside type="tip">
**Application of Concepts:** The Kraljic Matrix — referenced here for future sessions — classifies purchased items by supply risk and profit impact. It is a foundational tool for deciding whether to single-source, multi-source, or build strategic supplier partnerships, directly linking procurement decisions to SC responsiveness or efficiency.
</Aside>

## Driver 6 — Pricing

<Badge text="Cross-Sectional Driver" variant="caution" />

<Aside type="note">
**Key principle:** Customers who want responsiveness and customers who want efficiency are entirely different market segments. The right pricing strategy must target the right segment.
</Aside>

| Pricing Strategy | How It Works | SC Type |
|---|---|---|
| **Economies of Scale Pricing** | Suitable for large batch production; cost-effective, reduces changeover cost | Efficient SC |
| **EDLP (Everyday Low Pricing)** | Price stays steady over time; predictable demand signal | Efficient SC |
| **Fixed Pricing** | Set, unchanging prices | Efficient SC |
| **High-Low Pricing** | Different prices at different times through discounts and promotions; creates dynamic demand | Responsive SC |
| **Menu Pricing** | Price varies by response time, delivery location, or product attributes | Responsive SC |

## Master Summary — All 6 Drivers (Exam-Ready)

| Driver | Responsive SC | Efficient SC |
|---|---|---|
| **Facilities** | Decentralised, small capacity, flexible production, cross-docking warehouse | Centralised, large capacity, dedicated production, storage-based warehouse |
| **Inventory** | High FG inventory placed closer to consumers | Centralised FG inventory with order consolidation |
| **Transportation** | Rapid modes (Air), direct shipping; high-value, low-demand products | Slow modes (Sea), economies of scale; low-value, high-demand products |
| **Information** | Real-time visibility, demand sensing, ML methods, scenario planning, pull system | Forecasting, inventory management, production planning, push system |
| **Sourcing** | Supplier collaboration, alternate supplier base, customisation strategies | Cost optimisation, economies of scale, supplier consolidation, outsourcing |
| **Pricing** | High-low pricing, menu pricing (varies by time / location) | Everyday Low Pricing, fixed pricing, economies of scale pricing |
