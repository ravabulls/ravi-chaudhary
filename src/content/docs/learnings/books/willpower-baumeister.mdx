## Monthly Trend — Year-on-Year Overlay

### Understanding the problem first

What you are describing is a **year-on-year monthly overlay** — one of the most analytically powerful views in any operational dashboard. The reason it is so valuable for your context specifically:

Your annual horizon view answers "are we improving year over year?" The monthly overlay answers something much more nuanced: "is the improvement consistent across all months, or are we being saved by one or two good months?" For a cruise fleet this matters enormously — July and August look very different from December and January in terms of routes sailed, occupancy, and fuel behaviour.

**KPIs that genuinely benefit from monthly granularity:**

`kgCO₂e/ALBD` is the most important one monthly. It reveals whether seasonal patterns exist (repositioning voyages in certain months drive intensity up), whether improvements are consistent or lumpy, and gives early warning if the current year is tracking worse than last year well before the annual number is final.

`Fuel kg/ALBD` monthly shows operational efficiency separate from fuel type switching. If this is improving but kgCO₂e/ALBD is not, the fuel mix is the problem, not operational behaviour.

`HFO share %` and `LNG share %` monthly show exactly when in the year fuel switching is happening and whether it is consistent. Winter months often show MGO spikes near port emission control areas.

`Total tCO₂e` monthly lets you see if emission peaks are seasonal or structural — important for ETS liability forecasting.

`Operational revenue days %` monthly catches dry-dock clustering effects. A poor intensity month often coincides with fewer operational days, not worse operations.

**Key insights the monthly view reveals that annual cannot:**

- If December and January are always the highest-intensity months across every year, that is a structural seasonality effect from repositioning voyages with lower occupancy. If December 2026 is already below December 2025, you are ahead of pace.
- If August shows an HFO share spike every year, it may correlate with a specific high-traffic route where LNG bunkering is unavailable. That is an actionable operational finding.
- Overlaying intensity alongside operational days % explains whether a bad month is operationally bad or structurally bad (dry-dock, fewer denominators).
- Fleet CDE vs Fleet ABC with the fleet toggle: if CDE shows a consistent gap in Dec–Feb but narrows in summer months, the fuel type matters more in winter when weather routing is less flexible.

---

### The solution

A **dedicated new page with a dynamic KPI selector measure and FY_Year in the legend of a line chart**. No Power Query changes. No new relationships. One new disconnected table and four new DAX measures.

It works because your data already has `FY_Month` (1–12), `FY_Month_Name` (Dec–Nov), and `FY_Year` as separate columns. Power BI's line chart puts `FY_Month_Name` on the x-axis, `FY_Year` in the legend, and your KPI measure as the value — it automatically draws one line per year, scoped to that year's monthly data.

---

## Step-by-step implementation

### Step 1 — Create the KPI Selector table

A small disconnected reference table. No relationship to any other table. Its only job is to power a slicer that changes what the chart shows.

In Power BI Desktop: **Modeling → New table** → paste exactly:

```
KPI_Selector = 
DATATABLE(
    "KPI_Name", STRING,
    "KPI_Sort", INTEGER,
    {
        {"kgCO2e / ALBD",        1},
        {"gCO2e / ALB-Km",       2},
        {"Fuel kg / ALBD",       3},
        {"tCO2e total (000s)",   4},
        {"ALBDs commercial",     5},
        {"HFO share %",          6},
        {"LNG share %",          7},
        {"Op. revenue days %",   8}
    }
)
```

**After creating the table — set the sort order (required):**

Without this the slicer shows KPIs alphabetically, not in the intended order. Go to **Data view** → click the `KPI_Selector` table → click the `KPI_Name` column → **Column Tools ribbon → Sort by Column → KPI_Sort**.

Do **not** draw any relationship from this table to other tables.

---

### Step 2 — Create the dynamic selector measure

Right-click your `_Measures` table → **New measure** → paste:

```
Selected_KPI_Monthly = 
VAR _choice = SELECTEDVALUE(KPI_Selector[KPI_Name], "kgCO2e / ALBD")
RETURN
SWITCH(
    _choice,
    "kgCO2e / ALBD",       [KPI_kgCO2e_per_ALBD],
    "gCO2e / ALB-Km",      [KPI_gCO2e_per_ALBKm],
    "Fuel kg / ALBD",      [KPI_FuelCons_kg_per_ALBD],
    "tCO2e total (000s)",  [Total_tCO2e] / 1000,
    "ALBDs commercial",    [Total_ALBDs] / 1000,
    "HFO share %",         [FuelShare_HFO_Pct],
    "LNG share %",         [FuelShare_LNG_Pct],
    "Op. revenue days %",
        DIVIDE(
            SUM(Monthly_Operations_Master[Operational_Rev_Days]),
            SUM(Monthly_Operations_Master[Month_Days]),
            BLANK()
        ) * 100,
    [KPI_kgCO2e_per_ALBD]
)
```

The `SELECTEDVALUE` second argument (`"kgCO2e / ALBD"`) is the default — if nothing is selected in the slicer, the chart shows kgCO2e/ALBD rather than going blank. The last line before the closing parenthesis is also the SWITCH default, serving the same purpose.

---

### Step 3 — Create the dynamic Y-axis title measure

Without this the axis label stays static ("Selected_KPI_Monthly") regardless of which KPI is selected. Right-click `_Measures` → **New measure**:

```
Selected_KPI_Label = 
SELECTEDVALUE(KPI_Selector[KPI_Name], "kgCO2e / ALBD")
```

You wire this into the chart in Step 8.

---

### Step 4 — Create the month-on-month delta measure

This powers the delta table below the chart — it shows how the current year compares to the prior year for each month.

```
Monthly_Delta_vs_PriorYear = 
VAR _currentYear =
    VALUE(SELECTEDVALUE(Monthly_Operations_Master[FY_Year]))
VAR _priorYearKPI =
    CALCULATE(
        [Selected_KPI_Monthly],
        FILTER(
            ALL(Monthly_Operations_Master[FY_Year]),
            VALUE(Monthly_Operations_Master[FY_Year]) = _currentYear - 1
        )
    )
RETURN
[Selected_KPI_Monthly] - _priorYearKPI
```

`VALUE()` is required on `FY_Year` comparisons because the column comes through as text type from Power Query. Without it the arithmetic `= _currentYear - 1` fails silently and returns BLANK for every row.

This measure returns BLANK when multiple years are selected (because `SELECTEDVALUE` returns BLANK on multiple values). That is intentional — filter the delta table visual to a single year using a visual-level filter.

---

### Step 5 — Create the delta direction measure

The delta's colour direction is KPI-dependent: for kgCO2e a negative delta (lower than last year) is good and should be green. For ALBDs a positive delta (more days than last year) is good and should be green. Without this measure, conditional formatting is hardcoded to one direction and misleads on ALBDs or Op. Days.

```
Monthly_Delta_Direction = 
VAR _kpi =
    SELECTEDVALUE(KPI_Selector[KPI_Name], "kgCO2e / ALBD")
VAR _delta = [Monthly_Delta_vs_PriorYear]
VAR _lowerIsBetter =
    _kpi = "kgCO2e / ALBD"      ||
    _kpi = "gCO2e / ALB-Km"     ||
    _kpi = "Fuel kg / ALBD"     ||
    _kpi = "tCO2e total (000s)" ||
    _kpi = "HFO share %"
RETURN
IF(
    ISBLANK(_delta),
    BLANK(),
    IF(
        _lowerIsBetter,
        IF(_delta < 0, 1, -1),
        IF(_delta > 0, 1, -1)
    )
)
```

Returns `1` (good) or `-1` (bad) or BLANK. Wire this into the delta column's conditional formatting in Step 11 — rules-based: if value = 1 → background `#70AD47` (green), if value = -1 → background `#E03A3E` (red).

---

### Step 6 — Set the sort order for FY_Month_Name

Confirm this is already set (it should be from the blueprint, but verify). In Data view: click `Monthly_Operations_Master` table → click `FY_Month_Name` column → **Column Tools → Sort by Column → FY_Month**.

This ensures Dec appears first on the x-axis (FY_Month = 1), then Jan, Feb… through Nov (FY_Month = 12). Without this Power BI sorts alphabetically — Apr before Aug before Dec — completely wrong.

---

### Step 7 — Add a new report page

Right-click the page tabs at the bottom → **Add page** → rename it **Monthly Trend — YoY**.

---

### Step 8 — Add the KPI selector slicer

**Insert → Slicer** → drag `KPI_Selector[KPI_Name]` into the field well.

Format it:
- Format pane → Slicer settings → Style → **Tile** (clickable button tiles, one per KPI)
- Header title: `Select KPI`
- Single-select only: Format pane → Selection → **Multi-select with Ctrl → Off**
- Default: click the slicer on the canvas → click `kgCO2e / ALBD` once to set it as the loaded state

**Do not sync this slicer to other pages.** Check View → Sync slicers and confirm it is page-local.

---

### Step 9 — Add the year multi-select slicer

**Insert → Slicer** → drag `Monthly_Operations_Master[FY_Year]` into the field well.

Format it:
- Style → Tile or vertical list
- Multi-select enabled (users can overlay 2019 + 2024 + 2026 simultaneously)
- Default: select all available years so the chart loads fully populated

**Do not sync this slicer to other pages.** The horizon view on the executive page handles year filtering separately. If `FY_Year` is already synced from a previous slicer on another page, this new slicer is still page-local by default unless you explicitly add it to the sync group.

---

### Step 10 — Build the main line chart

**Insert → Line chart** (not bar — line is correct for time series comparison).

Wire it up:

| Field well | Value |
|---|---|
| X axis | `Monthly_Operations_Master[FY_Month_Name]` |
| Y axis | `[Selected_KPI_Monthly]` |
| Legend | `Monthly_Operations_Master[FY_Year]` |
| Tooltips | `[Selected_KPI_Label]` (optional — shows KPI name on hover) |

**Wire the dynamic Y-axis title:**
Format pane → Y axis → Title → toggle ON → under Title text, click the **fx** (field value) button → select `[Selected_KPI_Label]`. The axis label now changes automatically when the user picks a different KPI.

**Set per-year line colours manually:**
Format pane → Lines → expand → under **Apply settings to**, use the dropdown to select each year series and set its colour:

| Year | Colour | Style |
|---|---|---|
| 2019 | `#FFC000` amber | Dashed — signals baseline reference |
| 2023 | `#9DC3E6` muted blue | Solid |
| 2024 | `#4472C4` medium blue | Solid |
| 2025 | `#70AD47` green | Solid |
| 2026 | `#E03A3E` red | Solid, slightly thicker (2.5pt) — stands out as current year |

To set dashed: in that same per-series format panel, find **Stroke dash type → Dashed**.

**Add reference lines** (Analytics pane — the magnifying glass icon in the Format panel):
- Constant line → Value: `[your 2026 target value]` → Label: `2026 target` → colour `#FFC000`, dashed
- Constant line → Value: `[your 2030 PROPEL target value]` → Label: `PROPEL 2030` → colour `#E03A3E`, dotted

These lines run across all 12 months, making it immediately visible which monthly values have cleared the target and which have not.

**Turn off the default legend title:** Format → Legend → Title → Off (it would otherwise show "FY_Year" which looks untidy).

---

### Step 11 — Add the month delta table

**Insert → Table** visual. Wire it up:

| Column | Value |
|---|---|
| Row 1 | `Monthly_Operations_Master[FY_Month_Name]` |
| Row 2 | `[Selected_KPI_Monthly]` |
| Row 3 | `[Monthly_Delta_vs_PriorYear]` |
| Row 4 | `[Monthly_Delta_Direction]` (hidden — used only for conditional formatting) |

**Apply a visual-level filter** (not a slicer — visual-level only, so the line chart above is unaffected): expand Filters pane → Filters on this visual → drag `FY_Year` → filter to the single most recent year (e.g. 2026). The table always shows current year vs prior year delta, while the chart above can still show all years simultaneously.

**Apply conditional formatting to the delta column:**
Click the table visual → Format → select the delta column → Conditional formatting → Background color → **Format by: Field value → `[Monthly_Delta_Direction]`** → if value = 1 → `#70AD47` green, if value = -1 → `#E03A3E` red.

This works correctly for all KPIs — the direction measure already handles the sign logic per KPI. Hide the direction column itself: right-click column header → Hide.

---

### Step 12 — Wire the fleet slicer

The fleet slicer is already synced to all other pages. Go to **View → Sync slicers** → find the fleet slicer row → tick both **Sync** and **Visible** for this new page.

The fleet toggle in the top bar now controls this page too. Selecting Fleet ABC shows only ABC vessels' monthly data, Fleet CDE shows CDE's — so you can run the same analysis per fleet and compare.

---

### Step 13 — Final formatting

**Y-axis start value:** Set to a meaningful non-zero minimum rather than 0 — otherwise small but meaningful differences between years are visually compressed. Format → Y axis → Start → e.g. 60 for kgCO2e/ALBD (adjust to your actual data range). Note: this value is fixed and does not change when the KPI selector changes. Either accept this limitation or use a second dynamic min measure if precision matters.

**Partial year annotation:** Insert a text box near the current year's line or at the top of the page: `"2026 line shows data through latest available forecast month only."` This prevents misinterpretation when the 2026 line is shorter than prior year lines.

**Page background:** Same `#F2F6FA` as all other pages. Canvas size 1440 × 960.

**Reset button:** Insert → Button → Action: Bookmark → Default View (same pattern as all other pages). Set fleet slicer and year slicer to all-selected as the default state for this page's bookmark.

---

### What this page will show, practically

Once built, with kgCO2e/ALBD selected and all years shown:

If December and January are always the highest-intensity months across every year, that is a structural seasonality effect — repositioning voyages run with lower occupancy. If December 2026 is already below December 2025, you are ahead of pace and likely to meet the annual target.

With HFO share % selected: if August shows a spike every year, it may correlate with a specific high-traffic route or region where LNG bunkering is unavailable. Actionable operational finding.

With Op. revenue days % alongside kgCO2e/ALBD: if intensity spikes in a month with low op days, the cause is structural (fewer denominator ALBDs), not operational inefficiency. The two charts together explain the why.

With the fleet toggle: if CDE shows a consistent gap vs ABC in Dec–Feb but the gap narrows in summer months, the fuel type matters more in winter when weather routing is less flexible. Summer gains are operational; winter gap is structural.

The delta table tells you immediately: "June 2026 was 0.4 units worse than June 2025 — investigate this month." Red cell = action required. Green = on track for that month.
